# Manage assets

This topic describes how to manage assets.

## View asset details
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and click the asset name to open the details page.
3. On the `Basic` page (default), where you can view `Basic information` and more.
<img src="/images/docs/admin-guides/assets/asset-detail.png" alt="asset-detail" width="650"/>

## Edit asset properties and information
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and click the **Edit** in the `Actions` column. Alternatively, in the top-right corner of the asset details page, click **Edit**.
3. Change the properties or information that you want, and then click **Submit**.

## Delete an asset
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and select the **···** dropdown menu in the `Actions` column, then click **Delete**. Alternatively, in the top-right corner of the asset details page, click **Delete**.
3. Confirm the information to be deleted in the popup, and then click **OK**.

## Duplicate an asset
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and select the **···** dropdown menu in the `Actions` column, then click **Duplicate**.
<img src="/images/docs/admin-guides/assets/asset-duplicate.png" alt="asset-duplicate" width="300"/>
3. Modify the asset properties and information, and then click **Submit**.

## Enable or disable an asset
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and click the asset name to open the details page.
3. On the `Basic` page (default), locate the `Quick update` card on the right, then click **Active switch** to enable or disable the asset.
<img src="/images/docs/admin-guides/assets/asset-enable.png" alt="asset-enable" width="300"/>

## Refresh asset hardware information
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and click the asset name to open the details page.
3. On the `Basic` page (default), locate the `Quick update` card on the right, then click **Refresh** to start an Ansible task to update the asset’s hardware information.
<img src="/images/docs/admin-guides/assets/asset-refresh.png" alt="asset-refresh" width="300"/>
4. A new tab will open automatically to show the task execution logs.
5. After completion, view the asset’s hardware information in the `Hardware information` section on the left side of the asset details page.

## Test asset connection
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and select the **···** dropdown menu in the `Actions` column, then click **Test**. Alternatively, on the asset details "Basic" page, locate the `Quick update` card on the right and click **Test**.
<img src="/images/docs/admin-guides/assets/asset-test.png" alt="asset-test" width="300"/>
4. A new tab will open automatically to show the task execution logs.
5. Refresh the Assets list to view the asset’s connectivity status.

## Add or remove nodes for an asset
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and click the asset name to open the details page.
3. On the `Basic` page (default), locate the `Node` card on the right. 
4. Select one or more nodes, and you can search by node name, then click **Add**. 
<img src="/images/docs/admin-guides/assets/asset-add-node.png" alt="asset-add-node" width="300"/>
5. Click **-** icon to **Remove** the node.

## Add or remove tags for an asset
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and click the asset name to open the details page.
3. On the `Basic` page (default), locate the `Tags` card on the right. 
4. Select one or more tags, and you can search by tag name or value, then click **Add**. 
<img src="/images/docs/admin-guides/assets/asset-add-tag.png" alt="asset-add-tag" width="300"/>
5. Click **-** icon to **Remove** the tags.

## Manage asset accounts
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and click the asset name to open the details page.
3. On the `Accounts` page, you can see all accounts of the asset and perform the following actions.
    <img src="/images/docs/admin-guides/assets/asset-manage-account.png" alt="asset-manage-account" width="650"/>
    * **Add account**: Click **+ Create** to add a new account, or select **Account templates** to add an account from a template.
    * **View account secret**: Click **View** in the `Actions` column to view the account secret, but this step requires verifying the current user.
    * **More actions**: Select the **···** dropdown menu in the `Actions` column to **Edit, Test, Clear secret,** or **Delete** an account.
    * **Batch test**: In the `Test` card on the right side of the page, click **Batch test** to test the connectivity of all accounts.

## View authorized users of an asset
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and click the asset name to open the details page.
3. On the `Authorized users` page, you can view all **users** and **user groups** authorized for the asset.
4. Click **View** to see the authorization rules for the current user or user group.
<img src="/images/docs/admin-guides/assets/asset-authorized-user-view.png" alt="asset-authorized-user-view" width="550"/>

## View asset session records
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and click the asset name to open the details page.
3. On the `Asset sessions` page, you can view the session records of the asset.

## View asset command execution logs
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and click the asset name to open the details page.
3. On the `Commands` page, you can view the command execution logs of the asset.

## View asset activity logs
1. In JumpServer, navigate to **Console > Assets**.
2. In the Assets list, locate the relevant asset and click the asset name to open the details page.
3. On the `Activities` page, you can view the last 30 activity logs of the asset, including asset updates, logins, task executions, and more.
