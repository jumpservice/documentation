# create risk account detection task <Icon name="xpack" />

This topic describes Risk detection configurations.

## Prerequisites
At least one account must be added to the asset before processing Risk detection result. Automation tasks will prioritize accounts marked as privileged for execution.
Recomanded account privilege requirement are as follows:

<HorizontalTable 
headers={['Asset Type', 'Recomanded account privilege requirement']} 
rows={[
    ["Linux", "Root or sudoers"], 
    ["Windows", "Account in Administrator group"], 
    ["Unix", "Root or user with ALL PRIVILEGES"], 
    ["Mongodb", "User with 'userAdminAnyDatabase' or 'userAdmin' role"], 
    ["Mysql/MariaDB", "Root or user with ALL PRIVILEGES'"], 
    ["Postgresql", "Postgres or other superuser"], 
    ["Oracle", "DBA"],
    ["SQLServer", "Membership in 'sysadmin'"]
]}
/>

For **Minimum account privilege requirement** of each automation template. See [Platform settings](/docs/platform-settings/automation-settings).

## Create a risk detection task

<Steps>

<div>
<Nav name="pam" />
</div>

<div>
In the left menu, click **<Icon name="riskDetection" /> Risk detection**.
</div>

<div>
On the right page, click **Detect tasks** tab.
</div>

<div>
Above the table, click **Create**.
</div>

<div>
Type a **Name** for the task.
</div>

<div>
Optionally, choose **Assets nodes** to discover accounts for. All assets under the selected nodes will be included.
</div>

<div>
Optionally, choose **Assets** to discover accounts for.
</div>

<div>
Optionally, select risk detect Engine for the task.
<HorizontalTable 
headers={['Engines', 'Descripition']} 
rows={[
    ["Check the discovered accounts", "Perform checks and analyses based on automatically discovered account results,including user groups, public keys, sudoers, and other information."], 
    ["Check the strength of your account and password", "Perform checks and analyses based on the security of account passwords, includingpassword strength, leakage, etc."], 
    ["Check if the account and password are repeated", "Check if the account is the same as other accounts."], 
    ["Check whether the account password is a common password", "Check whether the account password is a commonly leaked password."]
]}
/>
</div>

<div>
Optionally, choose a **Recipient** for the notification. If enabled, the report will be sent to the recipient.
</div>

<div>
Optionally, disable or enable **Periodic**, where you can set the **Crontab** or **Interval** to schedule the task. 
If both interval and crontab are set, crontab is prioritized.
</div>

<div>
Optionally, disable or enable **Active**, which determines whether the task is active or not.
</div>

<div>
Optionally, type a **Description** for the task.
</div>

<div>
Click **Submit**.
</div>

</Steps>

## Review Detect results

<Steps>

<div>
After the risk detection task is completed, go to the **Execution result** tab.
</div>

<div>
In the table, locate the certain account. 
</div>

<div>
In the Pending column, click the <Icon name="greenHook" /> button to handle the risk detection results or <Icon name="greyBan" /> to ignore the risk detection results.
</div>

</Steps>
